W-8BEN Generator FAQs
---------------------

### Who should use the W‑8BEN Generator?



Non‑U.S. individual creators and contractors (not entities) who earn U.S‑sourced income and need to certify their foreign status and, if eligible, claim treaty benefits.

### Is the digitally‑signed form really accepted by the IRS and payors?



Yes. The IRS allows scanned or electronically signed W‑8BEN forms as long as every required field is complete and the signature is legible. Major payment platforms and finance teams recognize the PDF our W-8BEN Form Generator produces.

### How long is my W‑8BEN valid?



Three calendar years, ending on December 31 of the third year after the signing year (e.g., a form signed on May 23 2025 stays valid through December 31 2028). The generator tracks this window and sends renewal reminders 90, 30, and 7 days before expiry.

### What if my information changes after I submit the form?



Simply reopen Otto AI W-8BEN form generator, update the affected fields (such as address, citizenship, or tax ID), and re‑sign. The tool generates a fresh PDF and audit‑log entry so payors can replace the outdated version and stay compliant.

### Does the generator help me check whether my country has a tax treaty with the United States?



Absolutely. When you enter your country of tax residence, the wizard automatically looks up the applicable treaty rate (if one exists) and walks you through completing Part II. If your country has no treaty—or you choose not to claim benefits—the UI clearly explains the default 30 % withholding rate.
