What is Effective Tax Rate?
---------------------------

Your **Effective Tax Rate** represents the **average percentage of your taxable income that you pay in taxes** over the course of the year. Unlike the **marginal tax rate**, which only applies to the highest portion of your income, the effective rate provides a broader view of your [tax liability](https://joinotto.com/tax/tax-liability).

**Example:**

Let‚Äôs say you earned $50,000 in a year and paid $5,000 in taxes.

Your **Effective Tax Rate = $5,000 √∑ $50,000 = 10%**.

This means that 10% of your total income went to taxes‚Äînot 22% or 24% as your marginal bracket might suggest.

The effective tax rate offers a more accurate picture of your **actual tax burden**.

### How is Effective Tax Rate Calculated?

To determine your ETR, follow these three straightforward steps:

1.   **Determine Your Total Taxable Income**

This includes wages, freelance income, dividends, [capital gains](https://joinotto.com/tax/capital-gains-tax), rental income, and any other taxable sources. Be sure to subtract deductions and exemptions to get your _adjusted taxable income_.
2.   **Add Up Your Total Taxes Paid**

Include all federal income taxes paid, plus any applicable state income taxes, [self-employment taxes](https://joinotto.com/blog/self-employment-taxes), or other direct tax liabilities.
3.   **Use the Formula:**‚Äç

Effective Tax Rate = Total Taxes Paid √∑ Total Income.

This method helps you see the overall percentage of your income that is going to taxes, rather than just focusing on the highest rate you may pay on a portion of your income.

*   **Compare and Benchmark**

Your ETR provides a way to compare your tax burden with others in similar income brackets or professions. This can help evaluate whether your tax situation is fair or optimized.
*   **Prepare for Changes**

### **Common Misconceptions About Effective Tax Rate**

*   **It‚Äôs Not the Same as Your Tax Bracket**

Many people confuse their marginal tax rate (the rate applied to your last dollar of income) with their effective tax rate. In reality, your ETR is almost always lower than your marginal rate.
*   **It Includes More Than Just Federal Income Tax**

While often calculated using only federal taxes, some individuals and businesses include _state taxes, payroll taxes,_ and even _self-employment taxes_ for a more holistic picture.
*   **It Can Vary Widely by Income Type**

For instance, capital gains are often taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Someone with mostly investment income may have a lower ETR than someone earning a similar amount through wages.
