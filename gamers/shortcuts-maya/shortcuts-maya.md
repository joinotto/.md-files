Common Maya Shortcuts for Beginners
-----------------------------------

### Essential Navigation Shortcuts

*   Alt + Left Mouse Button: Rotate your view.
*   Alt + Middle Mouse Button: Pan the view.
*   Alt + Right Mouse Button: Zoom in and out.

Understanding navigation is fundamental, as it allows you to inspect your models from various angles effectively.

### Transform Shortcuts

*   W: Move Tool
*   E: Rotate Tool
*   R: Scale Tool

Familiarize yourself with these tools, as they are the backbone of modeling and animation in Maya.

Intermediate Shortcuts for Enhanced Workflow
--------------------------------------------

### Selecting and Painting Shortcuts

*   Ctrl + S: Save your current project.
*   Ctrl + D: Duplicate your selected object.
*   B: Toggle Sculpt Tool brush size.

These shortcuts will speed up your operations while working on complex scenes.

### Viewport and Camera Shortcuts

*   Spacebar: Toggle between full screen and normal view.
*   F: Frame the selected object in your viewport.

Using these shortcuts allows you to navigate your project more fluidly, which is particularly useful in large projects or when working with multiple elements.

Advanced Shortcuts for the Pro User
-----------------------------------

### Shelf and Hotbox Shortcuts

*   Shift + Ctrl + Alt + S: Open the Hotbox for quick access to menus.
*   Ctrl + Shift + K: Create a new shelf.

Advanced users can customize shelves and utilize Hotbox functions for rapid access to frequently used tools and commands, optimizing their workflow even further.

### Customizing Shortcuts

Did you know you can customize your Maya shortcuts?

1.   Go to Window > Settings/Preferences > Hotkey Editor.
2.   Assign your own keys for specific commands to match your workflow.

This personalization can make a significant difference in your productivity, tailoring the software to your needs.
