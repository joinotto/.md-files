Why Use Keyboard Shortcuts in SketchUp?
---------------------------------------

Using keyboard shortcuts increases your efficiency by minimizing the time spent navigating through menus. By memorizing frequently used commands, you can enjoy a more seamless design experience, allowing you to focus on your creative vision rather than getting bogged down by the interface.

Essential SketchUp Keyboard Shortcuts
-------------------------------------

### General Shortcuts

*   Select: **Spacebar** - Quickly switch to the Select tool to choose objects.
*   Undo: **Ctrl + Z** (Windows) / **Command + Z** (Mac) - Revert your last action.
*   Redo: **Ctrl + Y** (Windows) / **Command + Shift + Z** (Mac) - Restore an undone action.

### Navigation Shortcuts

*   Orbit: **O** - Allows you to change your view by orbiting around models.
*   Pan: **H** - Move your view horizontally or vertically.
*   Zoom: **Z** - Adjust the zoom level of your current view quickly.

### Drawing Shortcuts

*   Line Tool: **L** - Start drawing lines instantly.
*   Rectangle Tool: **R** - Quickly create rectangles.
*   Circle Tool: **C** - Draw perfect circles with ease.

### Modification Shortcuts

*   Move: **M** - Move selected objects to a new location.
*   Rotate: **Q** - Quickly rotate objects around their axes.
*   Scale: **S** - Adjust the size of selected objects effortlessly.

Customizing Your Shortcuts
--------------------------

SketchUp allows users to customize keyboard shortcuts to fit their unique workflow. Here’s how to customize:

1.   Go to Window > Preferences (Windows) or SketchUp > Preferences (Mac).
2.   Click on Shortcuts.
3.   Search for the command you’d like to change.
4.   Assign a new key combination and click Add.

This personalization ensures that the shortcuts align with your design habits, leading to an even more efficient use of the software.

Tips for Memorizing Shortcuts
-----------------------------

*   Practice Regularly: Regularly using shortcuts will help instill them in your muscle memory.
*   Create Cheat Sheets: Design custom cheat sheets that feature your favorite shortcuts for easy reference.
*   Use Mnemonics: Develop simple phrases to help remember complex shortcuts.
