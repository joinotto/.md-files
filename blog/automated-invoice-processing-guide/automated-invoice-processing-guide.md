What Is Automated Invoice Processing?
-------------------------------------

‍

Automated invoice processing refers to the use of technology—like artificial intelligence (AI), [optical character recognition (OCR)](https://joinotto.com/invoicing/invoice-ocr), and machine learning—to extract, verify, route, and store invoice data without manual input.

In short, it's a digital system that receives an invoice, reads the data, matches it against your accounting or ERP system, gets necessary approvals, and prepares it for payment—all without human touchpoints.

‍

_Think of it as putting your invoicing on autopilot._

‍

### The Problem With Manual Invoice Processing

Before diving into the tech, let’s be clear on what automated invoice processing is solving. Traditional invoice handling typically includes:

*   Receiving paper or PDF invoices via mail or email
*   Manually entering data into a spreadsheet or [accounting software](https://joinotto.com/accounting)
*   Matching with purchase orders or receipts
*   Manually forwarding for approvals
*   Chasing down late payments
*   Physically storing or archiving documents

### Risks of Manual Processes:

*   Human errors (typos, miscalculations)
*   Duplicate payments
*   Late fees or missed early payment discounts
*   Fraud risks
*   Low productivity and morale in finance teams

According to Stripe, nearly 80% of invoice-related AP errors stem from manual data entry.

How Automated Invoice Processing Works
--------------------------------------

Here’s a simplified breakdown of a modern automated invoice processing workflow:

**Step 1: Invoice Capture**

Invoices are received digitally (email, uploads, scanned paper) and imported into the automation system.

‍

**Step 2: Data Extraction**

OCR and AI extract key details from the invoice:

*   Vendor name
*   Date
*   Invoice number
*   Line items
*   Tax, total, and currency
*   PO reference (if available)

‍

**Step 3: Validation & Matching**

The system automatically matches the invoice against:

*   Purchase orders (2-way match)
*   Receipts or goods received notes (3-way match)

If discrepancies are found, it's flagged for review.

‍

**Step 4: Approval Routing**

Smart workflows route the invoice to the appropriate approver based on preset rules—like department, amount, or vendor.

‍

**Step 5: Posting & Payment**

Once approved, the invoice is pushed to your ERP or accounting system, and queued for payment.

‍

**Step 6: Archiving & Audit Trail**

Invoices are securely stored and easily searchable for future audits or vendor queries.

Automated Invoice Processing vs. Invoice Scanning
-------------------------------------------------

Some systems only scan and extract data—leaving the rest to manual follow-up.

‍

**Scanning Tools:**

*   Capture data from PDFs/images
*   Often require manual validation
*   Basic, not fully automated

**Full Automation:**

*   Extracts, validates, matches, routes, and archives
*   Reduces manual work to near zero
*   Integrates with back-end systems

‍

_Full automation goes beyond scanning—it thinks, routes, and acts._

#### Challenges to Watch Out For

Even automation has hurdles:

*   Poor quality scans or unstructured invoices
*   Resistance from teams used to manual processes
*   Compatibility issues with legacy ERP systems
*   Learning curve for rule setup and exception handling

But with proper planning and training, most teams adapt quickly.

‍

**Best Practices for Automated Invoice Processing**

*   Use standardized invoice templates to improve accuracy
*   Whitelist trusted vendors to fast-track approvals
*   Set up tiered approval workflows by dollar amount
*   Review monthly audit logs to catch outliers
*   Set reminders for expiring contracts or vendor terms
*   Choose tools with bank-grade security and compliance certifications

‍

**Alternatives If You're Not Ready to Fully Automate**

*   Not every business is ready for full invoice automation. Here are alternatives:
*   Semi-automated tools: Use invoice generators with auto-send and tracking.
*   Outsourced AP services: Let a third-party firm handle invoice processing.
*   Early-payment discounts: Encourage faster payments to reduce the invoice load.

But as your business scales, full automation pays for itself many times over.
