### How Your Florida Paycheck Works

In Florida, your job pays you either an hourly wage or an annual salary. However, your actual take-home pay will be less than the gross amount on your contract due to federal tax withholdings and potential deductions like health insurance or retirement contributions. Florida’s lack of state income tax simplifies things, but federal taxes still apply.

Key deductions from your paycheck include:

*   Federal Income Tax: Based on IRS tax brackets and your W-4 elections.
*   FICA Taxes: 6.2% for Social Security and 1.45% for Medicare. Employees earning over $200,000 (or joint filers over $250,000) pay an extra 0.9% Medicare surtax.
*   Other Pre-Tax Deductions: Contributions to health insurance, 401(k) plans, and other benefits reduce your taxable income.

Your employer also matches your FICA contributions. If you’re self-employed, you must pay both the employer and employee portions yourself—a combined 15.3% known as self-employment tax. Our florida paycheck calculator factors these in automatically.

Some paycheck deductions are mandatory, but others depend on choices you make:

*   Health Insurance: Selecting different health plans or adding dependents can increase or decrease your paycheck deductions.
*   W-4 Adjustments: Updating your W-4 form lets you control how much federal tax your employer withholds, which affects your paychecks and potential refunds.
