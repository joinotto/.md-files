### How Your Idaho Paycheck Works

Your paycheck starts with your gross earnings—your salary or hourly wage multiplied by hours worked. Idaho’s progressive income tax rates mean higher earners pay a greater percentage in state taxes. Your net pay is also reduced by federal income tax, FICA taxes, and any voluntary pre-tax deductions you choose.

Key deductions from your Idaho paycheck include:

*   Federal Income Tax: Calculated using IRS tax brackets and your W-4 elections.
*   Idaho State Income Tax: Ranges from 1% to 5.8% depending on your taxable income.
*   FICA Taxes: 6.2% Social Security tax and 1.45% Medicare tax, plus an additional 0.9% Medicare surtax for incomes over $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly).
*   Other Pre-Tax Deductions: Contributions to health insurance, 401(k), HSA, or other benefits.

Employers match Social Security and Medicare contributions. If you’re self-employed, you’re responsible for both the employee and employer shares—a combined 15.3% self-employment tax, which our idaho paycheck tax calculator includes automatically.

This means many Idahoans will earn an average monthly gross income over $6,000 in 2023. But your actual net pay will depend on taxes and deductions. Use our paycheck calculator Idaho to see your take-home pay clearly.

### How You Can Affect Your Idaho Paycheck

Some paycheck deductions are mandatory, but you can control others to adjust your net income:

*   Retirement Contributions: Increasing contributions to 401(k) or IRA accounts lowers your taxable income now but reduces your take-home pay.
*   Health Insurance & Benefits: Selecting more comprehensive health plans or adding dependents increases deductions.
*   W-4 Adjustments: Changing your W-4 affects how much federal tax your employer withholds, which impacts your paycheck size and future tax refunds.
