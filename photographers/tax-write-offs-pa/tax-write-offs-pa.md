Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lenses, lighting, computers, and gear used 100% for business are deductible. Capital items are depreciated, while low-cost items may be expensed. Section 179 allows immediate deduction; maintain receipts and depreciation logs.

$

Equipment Repairs & Maintenance

Routine equipment repairs and business software (like editing tools or subscriptions) are fully deductible. Multi-year licenses may be amortized. Deduct only the business-use portion if personal use exists. Save all invoices and receipts.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance PA
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in PA

Studio Rent & Home Office Expenses

Rent for studio or coworking space is deductible. Home office must be used exclusively and regularly. Deduct business-use share of rent, mortgage interest, taxes, and utilities. Keep records, floor plans, and utility bills.

$

Utilities (Electric, Internet, Phone)

Deduct studio or home office utilities like electricity, water, and internet. Full cost applies if business-only; otherwise, allocate business-use share. Keep monthly bills and note allocations. Internet, hotspot, and cell bills are also deductible.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Car/Truck Use)

Business use of a vehicle qualifies for deduction using actual costs or mileage rate. Maintain detailed mileage logs. Commuting isn't deductible. Choose the method offering the higher deduction and keep fuel and maintenance receipts.

$

Travel (Lodging,Transportation & Meals)

Deduct business travel including airfare, hotels, and meals. Allocate for personal time if combined. Meals require receipts or per diem (federal only). Document business purpose. Baggage fees, dry cleaning, and equipment shipping are deductible.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor & Hired Assistants

Payments to second shooters, editors, assistants, and freelancers are deductible. Issue Form 1099-NEC for anyone paid $600 or more. Keep contracts and payment records. Wages for employees are reported separately from contractor expenses.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education (Workshops & Courses)

Workshops, online courses, publications, and educational tools related to photography or business are deductible. Must maintain or improve current skills, not qualify for a new career. Save receipts and course descriptions for documentation.

$

Professional Subscriptions & Dues

Deduct fees for memberships in photography organizations and subscriptions to professional tools or publications. Allocate only the business-use portion for mixed-use items. Keep proof of payment and ensure its tied to your business.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website Cost
-------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotion

Deduct costs for print or online ads, flyers, event sponsorships, SEO, and logo design. Promotional gifts under $25 per client per year are allowed. Keep records of all advertising-related invoices and vendor receipts.

$

Website & Online Presence Costs

Web hosting, domains, design templates, platform subscriptions, and social media promotion tools are deductible. Website costs are typically expensed. Prepaid multi-year costs may be amortized. Keep receipts and designer agreements for documentation.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Deduct insurance premiums for liability, gear, property, and commercial auto policies. Personal health and life insurance are not deductible. Keep premium statements and allocate if any policies cover both business and personal assets.

$

Business Licenses, Permits & Fees

Fees for registering your business, permits for shoots, or professional licenses are deductible. This includes LLC filing fees and city-specific license costs. Maintain receipts and ensure these are directly related to your business.

$

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props, Backdrops & Consumables

Props, furniture, backdrops, photo paper, batteries, SD cards, and other consumables are deductible. Small, inexpensive tools are expensed in the year purchased. Deduct only the business-use share. Keep all purchase receipts and usage logs.

$

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank Charges & Merchant Fees

Bank account fees, credit card processing fees, and service charges from platforms like PayPal or Stripe are deductible. Allocate business-use portions if shared accounts are used. Maintain bank statements and processor fee summaries.

$

Interest on Business Loans

Deduct interest on business loans, credit cards, or financed equipment. Allocate mixed-use items based on business percentage. Prepaid interest must be prorated. Save statements, amortization schedules, and loan agreements for your records.

$

Other (Miscellaneous & General Deductions) PA
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for other miscellaneous and general deductions PA

Business Meals & Entertainment

Meals with clients, staff meals during work, and entertainment with a business purpose are deductible. Keep documentation showing date, attendees, location, and purpose. Gifts under $25 per client per year are also deductible.

$

Professional Services (Legal, Accounting, etc.)

Legal, accounting, tax prep (for Schedule C), consulting, and service provider fees are deductible. Exclude personal services. Maintain contracts and invoices for audit support. Categorize fees accurately based on service type and purpose.

$

Startup Costs & One-Time Expenses

Deduct up to $5,000 in startup costs; amortize excess over 15 years. Includes pre-launch marketing, training, and setup costs. Also includes one-time events like studio moves or business access upgrades. Keep clear expense documentation.
