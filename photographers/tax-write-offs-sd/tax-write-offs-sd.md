Equipment and Gear
------------------

Cameras, Lenses, and Accessories

South Dakota photographers can deduct 100% of cameras, lenses, filters, and stabilizers in the year of purchase using Section 179 expensing (up to $1.25 million in 2025) or depreciate them over 5–7 years via Form 4562. The state fully conforms to federal bonus depreciation rules, allowing immediate write-offs for equipment used >50% for business. Drones for aerial photography qualify if used primarily for commercial work.

$

Lighting Systems and Backdrops

Studio lighting (strobes, LED panels), softboxes, collapsible backdrops, and replacement bulbs are deductible. South Dakota exempts sales tax on equipment purchases if you provide vendors with a resale certificate (Form ST-121). Track small recurring costs (e.g., bulbs) under "Supplies".

$

Computers and Editing Hardware

Computers, monitors, and external hard drives used for editing are deductible. If used personally, only the business percentage (e.g., 80%) qualifies. South Dakota taxes hardware at 4.2% sales tax unless purchased for resale.

Studio and Home Office
----------------------

Studio Rent and Utilities

Deduct 100% of rent, electricity, and internet for dedicated studios. For mixed-use spaces, deduct the business percentage (e.g., 30% of a live/work loft). South Dakota requires no additional forms beyond federal Form 8829.

$

Home Office Simplified Deduction

Claim $5/sq ft (up to 300 sq ft) without itemizing expenses. South Dakota recognizes home studios if they meet IRS exclusivity tests. Soundproofing panels and ergonomic chairs qualify as improvements.

$

Travel and Transportation
-------------------------

Business Mileage

Use the 2025 federal rate (67¢/mile) or actual expenses (gas, repairs). Maintain a mileage log with dates, destinations, and purposes (e.g., "Client shoot at Badlands National Park").

$

Lodging and Meals

Deduct 100% of lodging for overnight business trips. Meals are 50% deductible (100% if temporary federal provisions apply). National Park permit fees (e.g., Custer State Park) are deductible as "Licensing Costs".

$

Professional Development
------------------------

Workshops and Certifications

FAA Part 107 drone licenses, SEO courses, and workshop fees are deductible. South Dakota exempts sales tax on educational materials purchased for resale (e.g., workshop handouts).

$

Trade Subscriptions

Deduct memberships to Professional Photographers of America (PPA) and subscriptions to Shutter Magazine. These qualify as "Continuing Education" under IRS guidelines.

$

Marketing and Client Expenses
-----------------------------

Website and Digital Advertising

Domain fees, hosting, Google Ads, and SEO tools (Ahrefs) are deductible. South Dakota taxes physical marketing materials (brochures) at 4.2% but exempts digital ad spend.

$

Client Gifts and Packaging

Deduct up to $25 per client annually for gifts (e.g., branded USB drives). Luxury packaging (linen boxes) is fully deductible as a "Cost of Goods Sold".

State-Specific Tax Laws
-----------------------

Sales Tax on Digital vs. Tangible Products

South Dakota taxes physical prints/albums at 4.2% + local rates but exempts digital downloads unless classified as prewritten software. Use resale certificates for frames/albums bought for resale.

$

Use Tax Compliance

Equipment purchased out-of-state (e.g., B&H Photo) incurs 4.2% South Dakota use tax if no sales tax was paid. Report via Form 1350 if annual purchases exceed $100.

$

School Photography Exemptions

Sales to accredited schools are tax-exempt if payment comes directly from the institution. Photographers must retain the school’s exemption certificate.

$

IRS Compliance
--------------

Depreciation vs. Section 179

Use Form 4562 to elect Section 179 (immediate expensing) or depreciate gear over 5–7 years. SUVs over 6,000 lbs (e.g., Chevy Tahoe) qualify for $31,300 first-year depreciation.

$

Record-Keeping Requirements

Retain receipts, mileage logs, and contracts for 3 years. Digital timestamps (e.g., Lightroom metadata) are valid proof for audits.

Legal and Financial
-------------------

LLC Annual Fees

South Dakota’s $50 LLC annual report fee is deductible. S Corps can deduct 20% of qualified business income (QBI) through 2025.

$

Tax Automation Tools

QuickBooks Online and TurboTax Self-Employed subscriptions are deductible. South Dakota exempts sales tax on cloud-based accounting software.

Federal & State Interaction

QBI Deduction: 20% of pass-through income (expires 2025). Economic Nexus: Register for sales tax if earning >$100k annually. No Business Personal Property Tax: South Dakota exempts equipment from annual property taxes.
