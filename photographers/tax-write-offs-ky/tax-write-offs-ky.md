Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Photographers can deduct cameras, drones, lights, and gear under Section 179. Kentucky conforms to federal depreciation rules but does not allow bonus depreciation. Keep detailed receipts and asset logs for IRS and KY Department of Revenue records.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Rentals

Gear repairs, cleanings, and rentals (like lenses or backdrops) are fully deductible. Kentucky mirrors federal treatment as ordinary expenses. Use Schedule C lines 16 or 20. Save receipts and document use in photography jobs.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Studio Rent, Utilities & Office Expenses

Renting a studio or shared creative space is deductible, along with utilities like electricity, internet, and phone. Kentucky allows full deduction for exclusive business use. Report on Schedule C, Lines 20–25. Keep contracts and invoices.

$

Home Office Deduction (In-home Studio)

Deduct a portion of rent, utilities, and home insurance if you use a space exclusively for business. Kentucky follows federal guidelines including the simplified method ($5/sq ft) or actual cost approach. File IRS Form 8829.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Use (Mileage & Auto Expenses)

You can deduct business mileage or actual vehicle expenses like gas, insurance, and repairs. Kentucky accepts federal methods. Excise tax on business vehicles is also deductible. Keep detailed mileage logs and expense records.

$

Business Travel (Airfare, Hotels & Meals)

Travel for photography jobs or training—such as airfare, hotels, and 50% of meals—is deductible. Kentucky follows federal rules. Separate personal travel from business. Save receipts and document trip purpose and client connections.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Freelancers & Independent Contractors

Hiring second shooters, editors, or assistants is deductible. Kentucky mirrors federal reporting requirements—issue 1099-NEC for contractors paid $600 or more. Keep W-9s, contracts, and payment records for audit protection.

$

Employee Wages & Payroll Taxes

Wages, employer-paid payroll taxes, and benefits are deductible if you hire employees. Kentucky requires proper withholding and reporting (e.g., KY Withholding Form K-1). Sole proprietor’s own labor is not deductible.

$

Education & Professional Development
------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education & Training

Costs for online courses, in-person workshops, and photography business classes are deductible if they enhance your skills. Kentucky conforms with federal tax law. Document how training supports your business.

$

Professional Memberships

Membership fees for organizations like PPA or local photography groups are deductible. Kentucky allows these under ordinary and necessary business expenses. Avoid deducting political or social club dues.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website
--------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotion

Expenses for print ads, social media ads, flyers, and promotional giveaways are deductible. Kentucky follows federal guidelines. Deduct these costs under advertising. Limit client gifts to $25 per recipient.

$

Website, SEO & Branding

Website design, domain names, hosting, and SEO tools are deductible. Kentucky treats these as marketing or software expenses. Ongoing costs may be deducted annually; large projects may be amortized.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for general liability, gear protection, or commercial property insurance are fully deductible. Kentucky allows these as ordinary business expenses. Report on Schedule C Line 15 and keep insurance policy records.

$

Business Licenses & Permits

Fees for Kentucky business licenses, city permits, or location shooting permissions are deductible. Include franchise taxes or certification costs. However, state income taxes and fines are not deductible.

$

Props, Backdrops & Supplies
---------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props & Studio Materials

Props like backdrops, costumes, furniture, or décor used for photography sessions are deductible. Kentucky allows these as supplies or depreciable assets depending on value. Track business use and maintain receipts.

$

Consumable Supplies (USBs, Prints, Paper)

Ink, paper, USB drives, batteries, and cleaning wipes are all deductible. Kentucky aligns with federal treatment. Deduct under Schedule C Line 22. Save receipts and consider grouping purchases for accurate recordkeeping.

$

Banking, Interest & Payment Processing
--------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Loan & Credit Card Interest (Business Use)

Interest on business credit cards or loans is deductible. Kentucky follows federal law. You must separate personal and business purchases. Mixed-use debt requires usage logs and itemized statements.

$

Banking & Processor Fees

Stripe, PayPal, Square, and bank service fees are deductible. Kentucky treats them as ordinary business expenses. Deduct under “Other expenses” and retain monthly reports and transaction logs.

$

Other (State-Specific or Niche Deductions)
------------------------------------------

Kentucky-specific and niche tax deductions

Self-Employment Tax Deduction

Deduct 50% of your federal self-employment tax. Kentucky does not allow a separate SE tax deduction on state returns, but follows the federal rule on Form 1040 Schedule 1.

$

Health Insurance Premiums

Self-employed photographers can deduct premiums for health, dental, or long-term care plans. Kentucky conforms with federal rules. Limit based on business income. Keep Form 1095 and insurer summaries.

$

Retirement Contributions (SEP, SIMPLE, Solo 401k)

Contributions to SEP IRAs or solo 401(k)s are deductible federally. Kentucky does not tax retirement contributions again, so no state-level add-back is required. Maintain contribution receipts for tax prep.

$

Startup Costs (For New Businesses)

Up to $5,000 in startup expenses can be deducted if you're launching a new freelance business. Kentucky follows federal rules. Deduct costs like branding, website setup, and legal fees in year one.
