Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, drones, computers, and lenses are deductible as business assets. Wisconsin follows federal Section 179 and MACRS rules but does not allow bonus depreciation. Keep receipts, depreciation logs, and allocate only business-use portions.

$

Equipment Repairs & Rentals

Repairs and gear rentals are fully deductible in the year paid. Keep invoices and rental contracts. Sales tax on rentals is deductible. These are treated as ordinary expenses on your Wisconsin Schedule C.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in Wisconsin

Studio Rent & Home Office Expenses

Studio or coworking space rent is fully deductible. Home office deductions follow federal rules (actual expenses or simplified method). Document the office’s square footage and expenses. Wisconsin accepts IRS Form 8829 inputs via Schedule C.

$

Office Utilities & Property Insurance

Electricity, internet, business phone lines, and property insurance are deductible. Allocate shared services by business-use percentage. Wisconsin aligns with federal treatment—no separate reporting is required if deducted on your federal return.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Car/Truck Use)

Business driving is deductible using the IRS mileage rate (65.5¢/mile in 2023) or actual costs. Commuting is not deductible. Wisconsin conforms to federal vehicle expense rules. Maintain logs and receipts.

$

Travel (Lodging, Transportation & Meals)

Business-related travel costs—flights, lodging, rental cars, and 50% of meals—are deductible. Wisconsin mirrors federal limits. Entertainment is not deductible. Document itineraries, receipts, and purpose of travel.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor & Hired Assistants

Payments to second shooters, editors, or assistants are deductible. Issue 1099-NEC if over $600/year. Wisconsin accepts these deductions as ordinary business expenses. Keep W-9s, contracts, and proof of business purpose.

$

Temporary Hires & Models

Short-term help like day assistants or stylists is deductible. If workers are employees, related payroll taxes are deductible too. Wisconsin requires proper worker classification. Maintain payroll or contractor documentation.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education (Workshops & Courses)

Workshops, webinars, and industry conferences are deductible if they improve your current business skills. Wisconsin follows federal rules—no extra limits. Maintain course receipts, registration, and evidence of business relevance.

$

Professional Memberships & Subscriptions

Dues for professional groups and photography-related subscriptions are deductible. Wisconsin allows full deduction if business-related. Political or personal portions are not allowed. Retain proof of payment and business justification.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website Costs
--------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotional Materials

Ad expenses including online ads, business cards, and client gifts (up to $25/client) are deductible. Wisconsin allows all federally deductible marketing costs. Save invoices and track ad spend carefully.

$

Website, Online Presence, and Branding Costs

Web design, hosting, domain fees, SEO, and branding costs are deductible. Wisconsin treats online marketing like traditional ads—fully deductible. Allocate mixed-use expenses and keep service receipts and contracts.

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Liability Insurance

Liability, E&O, and equipment insurance premiums are deductible. Wisconsin conforms to federal treatment. Health insurance is deducted elsewhere (not on Schedule C). Keep invoices and ensure policies are business-specific.

$

Licenses, Permits & Fees

Business licenses, park shoot permits, and drone registrations are deductible. Wisconsin allows full deduction, including the $20 seller’s permit fee. Maintain receipts for any local or state regulatory fees.

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props, Backdrops & Set Materials

Props and backdrops used in shoots are deductible. Expensive items may be depreciated or expensed via Section 179. Wisconsin allows deduction of sales tax paid. Keep receipts and document use.

$

Consumable Supplies (Printing, Office & Miscellaneous)

Supplies like printer ink, batteries, hard drives, and cloud storage are deductible. Categorize deliverables under COGS if resold. Wisconsin accepts all federal definitions and allows deduction of sales tax paid.

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Other Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank Charges & Payment Processing Fees

Business bank fees and payment processor charges (PayPal, Stripe, etc.) are deductible. Wisconsin follows federal rules—only business-related charges qualify. Keep statements and merchant fee records.

$

Interest on Business Loans and Credit

Interest on business loans, credit cards, and lines of credit is deductible. Wisconsin conforms to federal rules with no extra limits. Track interest separately and keep loan documentation.

$

Other Financial Expenses (Legal & Professional Fees)

Business-related legal, accounting, and consulting fees are deductible. Wisconsin mirrors federal treatment. LLC filing fees are deductible as start-up costs. Maintain invoices that describe the business purpose.

Other (Niche or State-Specific Deductions)
------------------------------------------

Deductions for other niche or Wisconsin-specific deductions

Home Office Deduction

Covered in the Studio Rent category. Wisconsin honors the federal deduction as filed on IRS Form 8829. No additional forms are required.

$

Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction (federal-only)

Wisconsin does not allow the federal QBI deduction (20% of net income). Your full business income is taxable for state purposes. Plan accordingly for higher taxable income.

$

Start-up and Organizational Expenses

Up to $5,000 of start-up and $5,000 of organizational costs are deductible federally and by Wisconsin. Excess amounts are amortized. Keep thorough records of pre-launch expenses.

$

Sales Tax Remitted on Income (Not Deductible)

Sales tax collected from clients is not income and not deductible. Wisconsin excludes this from gross receipts. Use taxes paid on purchases become part of your deductible expense cost.
