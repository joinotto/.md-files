Equipment and Gear Deductions
-----------------------------

Essential deductions for photography equipment, including cameras, lighting, computers, and accessories.

Camera Bodies and Lenses

Cameras, lenses, and related gear are deductible via Section 179 expensing (up to $1.25 million in 2025) or depreciation over 5–7 years. Kansas offers a 25% income tax credit for personal property tax paid on commercial equipment. Items under $1,500 per unit are exempt from property tax.

$

Lighting and Studio Equipment

Lighting kits, tripods, stands, and backdrops qualify as business expenses. Purchases of these items are subject to Kansas sales tax unless used directly in creating taxable goods (like prints). Deductible through Section 179 or depreciation.

$

Computers and Software

Computers, monitors, tablets, and photo editing software (such as Adobe Creative Cloud) are fully deductible. Kansas does not tax digital goods, so software subscriptions are sales-tax-exempt.

$

Photography Accessories and Consumables

Accessories such as memory cards, batteries, camera bags, cleaning supplies, and calibration tools are deductible as ordinary business expenses in the year purchased.

$

Studio and Office Space
-----------------------

Deductions for home offices, external studios, and related expenses for photographers.

Home Office Deduction

Photographers in Kansas can use the federal home office deduction if the space is used exclusively and regularly for business. Choose the simplified method ($5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft) or the regular method (percentage of home expenses). Use IRS Form 8829 for the regular method.

$

External Studio Rent

Rent paid for a dedicated photography studio outside the home is fully deductible. Utilities (electricity, water, internet) for the studio are also deductible. Kansas sales tax may apply to utilities unless specifically exempted.

$

Travel and Transportation
-------------------------

Deductions for business-related travel, mileage, and transportation expenses.

Mileage Deductions

Deduct business-related vehicle use using the IRS standard mileage rate (e.g., 67¢/mile for 2025) or actual expenses (fuel, maintenance, insurance). Commuting from home to your main studio is not deductible, but travel to client locations is.

$

Parking and Tolls

Parking fees and tolls incurred during business travel are 100% deductible. Keep detailed logs with dates, locations, and purposes.

$

Out-of-State Assignments

Airfare, lodging, rental cars, and 50% of meals are deductible when traveling for photography assignments outside your local area. If the trip is partly personal, only the business portion is deductible.

$

Subcontractors and Assistants
-----------------------------

Tax considerations for hiring help in your photography business.

Payments to Assistants and Second Shooters

Wages or fees paid to assistants, second shooters, or freelance editors are deductible business expenses. Issue a 1099-NEC to each contractor paid $600 or more in a year, as required by Kansas and federal law.

$

Education and Professional Development
--------------------------------------

Deductions for photography workshops, training, and professional certifications.

Workshops and Training

Fees for workshops, online courses, and seminars that improve or maintain your photography skills are deductible. Expenses that qualify you for a new profession are not deductible.

$

Professional Certifications

Costs for obtaining or maintaining professional certifications or licenses related to photography are deductible.

$

Marketing and Advertising
-------------------------

Deductions for promoting your photography business online and in print.

Website and Social Media

Costs for website design, hosting, domain registration, and online advertising (Google, Facebook, Instagram) are fully deductible. Kansas sales tax applies to printed marketing materials but not to digital advertising.

$

Branding and Printed Materials

Expenses for business cards, brochures, flyers, and other printed promotional materials are deductible. Kansas sales tax applies to the purchase of these physical items.

$

Insurance
---------

Deductions for business liability and equipment protection.

Business Liability and Equipment Insurance

Premiums for business liability insurance and insurance policies covering photography equipment are fully deductible. Kansas does not impose sales tax on insurance premiums.

$

Licensing, Permits, and Professional Fees
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for business licenses and professional memberships.

Business Licenses and Permits

Fees for business licenses, city permits, and professional association memberships are deductible as ordinary business expenses.

$

Props, Backdrops, and Consumable Supplies
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for photography session materials and supplies.

Props and Backdrops

Props, backdrops, and set materials purchased for use in photo sessions are deductible. These can be expensed in the year purchased as ordinary business supplies.

$

Consumable Supplies

Consumables such as photo paper, printer ink, packaging, and shipping supplies are deductible as ordinary and necessary business expenses.

$

Financial and Banking Expenses
------------------------------

Deductions for business banking fees and financing costs.

Bank Fees and Loan Interest

Business bank account fees, credit card processing fees, and interest paid on business loans or equipment financing are deductible.

Kansas-Specific Tax Laws and Credits
------------------------------------

State-specific tax considerations for photographers in Kansas.

Sales Tax on Tangible vs. Digital Goods

Kansas imposes a 6.5% state sales tax (plus local) on tangible products (prints, albums) but not on digital downloads (JPEGs, digital albums). Materials used in production (like photo paper) may be sales-tax-exempt if resold.

$

Equipment Property Tax Exemptions

Business equipment under $1,500 per unit is exempt from Kansas personal property tax. Equipment purchased after June 2006 for business expansion may also be exempt. See Kansas Department of Revenue for details.

$

Business Machinery and Equipment Credit

Kansas allows a 25% income tax credit for personal property tax paid on commercial equipment. Claim this on Schedule K-64.

$

Federal Tax Considerations
--------------------------

Important federal tax forms and considerations for photographers.

IRS Forms for Photographers

Use Schedule C (Form 1040) to report business income and expenses. Use Form 4562 for Section 179 expensing and depreciation. Use Form 8829 for the home office deduction (regular method).

$

Depreciation vs. Section 179 Expensing

Section 179 allows immediate expensing of equipment up to $1.25 million in 2025. If not using Section 179, depreciate equipment over 5–7 years. Choose the method that best suits your business's current and projected income.

$

Record-Keeping Requirements

Keep receipts, invoices, mileage logs, and contracts for at least three years. Digital record-keeping systems (like QuickBooks) are deductible.

$

LLC vs. Sole Proprietorship

LLCs offer liability protection and similar tax treatment as sole proprietors. S-corp election can reduce self-employment taxes for higher-earning photographers. Consult a tax professional for the best structure.
