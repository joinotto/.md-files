Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lenses, lighting kits, computers, and similar business equipment are deductible. You may depreciate over multiple years or fully expense via Section 179 (if used >50% for business). Nebraska follows federal limits and bonus depreciation rules.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Software

Routine maintenance (cleaning, tune-ups, part replacements) and software like Lightroom or Photoshop are deductible in full. Major upgrades may require depreciation. Nebraska honors these write-offs. Keep receipts and software license confirmations for audit protection.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in Nebraska

Studio or Office Rent

Rent paid for commercial studio space, co-working spots, or office leases is 100% deductible. Include related utilities and insurance. Nebraska does not tax commercial rent. Document lease terms and business-only usage for shared spaces.

$

Home Office (Business Use of Home)

If a room in your home is used exclusively and regularly for business, deduct a portion of rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance, and upkeep. Use IRS Form 8829 or simplified $5/sq ft method. Nebraska accepts federal-calculated deduction without changes.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Mileage & Auto Expenses

Choose between deducting the IRS standard mileage rate (65.5¢/mile in 2024) or actual costs (fuel, repairs, insurance). Nebraska accepts either. Track total vs. business miles with a mileage log or app. Business-use car loan interest is also deductible.

$

Business Travel (Out-of-Town)

Trips for shoots or conferences allow deduction of airfare, hotels, rental cars, and 50% of meals. Nebraska honors federal treatment. Sales tax on lodging or transport in Nebraska becomes part of the expense. Keep receipts and trip justifications.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor (Freelancers)

Payments to second shooters, editors, stylists, or virtual assistants are fully deductible. Issue a 1099-NEC if you pay $600+ per contractor yearly. Nebraska expects proper reporting and follows IRS rules. Retain W-9s and contracts or invoices.

$

Payroll (Employees)

If you hire W-2 employees, deduct wages, employer-paid taxes, and benefits. You must follow payroll rules. Nebraska includes these costs in state income calculation. Most freelancers stick to contract labor for flexibility and simplicity.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Courses, Conferences & Workshops

Classes or events that improve your existing photography skills are deductible. Includes in-person or online workshops, seminars, and related travel. Nebraska conforms to federal policy—these costs reduce state income too. Save syllabi or proof of course content.

$

Memberships & Subscriptions

Dues for groups like PPA, subscriptions to trade magazines or editing platforms, and paid learning platforms (e.g., Skillshare) are deductible. Nebraska recognizes them as ordinary business expenses, as long as they’re not political or lobbying-related.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website Costs
--------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website costs

Advertising & Branding

Print ads, flyers, logo design, business cards, and social media ad spend are all deductible. Nebraska mirrors IRS rules. Hiring a freelance designer for over $600? Issue a 1099-NEC. Track advertising receipts and ensure business purpose.

$

Website, Hosting & Online Tools

Website creation, hosting, domain fees, online portfolios (e.g., SmugMug), and email marketing tools (e.g., Mailchimp) are deductible. Nebraska doesn’t tax digital ad services, so no sales tax to worry about here. Save billing confirmations or screenshots.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Insurance Premiums

Deduct premiums for business liability, gear/equipment coverage, and studio property insurance. Nebraska permits full deductions and imposes no tax on premiums paid by small businesses. Keep policies and premium payment statements on file.

$

Licenses & Permits

City business licenses, home business permits, LLC registration, and special photography permits (like park shoots) are deductible. Nebraska’s sales tax permit is free, but fees paid to third-party filing services are deductible too. Maintain receipts.

$

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Studio Props & Set Materials

Furniture, backdrops, or décor used in shoots can be expensed or depreciated depending on cost and lifespan. Nebraska recognizes either method. Track purchases and usage. Use resale certificates for client-sold items to avoid sales tax upfront.

$

Consumables & Printing Supplies

Items like printer ink, photo paper, batteries, cleaning supplies, packaging, and snacks for clients are deductible. Nebraska allows deduction of sales or use tax paid. Maintain clear labeling of receipts (props vs. supplies vs. resale).

$

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank & Processing Fees

Monthly account fees, Stripe/PayPal fees, and ATM charges related to business are deductible. Nebraska follows federal treatment. Use a separate business account to avoid confusion and simplify documentation.

$

Interest on Business Loans

Interest on loans or credit cards used for business is deductible. Allocate only the business-use portion if accounts are mixed. Nebraska does not require any adjustment for business interest deductions. Save loan agreements and interest statements.

$

Professional & Legal Fees

Tax prep, bookkeeping, and legal service fees related to your business are deductible. Document the service and purpose.

Other (State-Specific Programs)
-------------------------------

Nebraska-specific credits and tax benefits

Nebraska Microenterprise Tax Credit

A refundable credit (up to $10,000/year, $20,000 lifetime) for increasing payroll or investments. Must apply before expanding. Covers new hires or purchases like gear/studios. Nebraska-only; no effect on federal taxes. Prepare documentation carefully to qualify.
