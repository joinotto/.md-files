Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lenses, drones, and lighting gear are deductible. MA follows Section 179 expensing but disallows bonus depreciation. Deduct or depreciate depending on purchase amount. Track use and file Form 4562 if applicable.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Rentals

Routine maintenance and rented gear (like lenses or printers) are deductible in full. MA treats these as standard business expenses. Keep invoices and explain business use on Schedule C (Lines 16, 20, or 26).

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in MA

Studio Rent, Utilities & Office Expenses

Rent for commercial space, co-working studios, and utilities like Wi-Fi or electricity are deductible. MA allows proportional deductions for mixed-use services. Report under Rent, Utilities, or Office Expenses on Schedule C.

$

Home Office (In-home Studio) Deduction

MA allows the $5/sq.ft. safe harbor or actual cost method. Deduct a portion of rent, utilities, insurance, or mortgage interest if the space is exclusively used for business. Claim on Schedule C, Line 29.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Use (Mileage & Auto Expenses)

Use either the IRS mileage rate or actual vehicle expenses. MA accepts both methods but disallows bonus depreciation. Deduct excise tax for business use. Maintain mileage logs and proof of business purpose.

$

Business Travel (Airfare, Hotels & Meals)

MA follows federal rules: travel for client work or conferences is deductible. Lodging, airfare, rideshares, and 50% of meals are allowed. Keep detailed receipts and itineraries. Exclude personal travel days.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor & Freelance Assistants

Payments to second shooters, editors, or stylists are fully deductible. MA requires 1099s for non-corporate contractors paid $600+. Maintain W-9s, payment records, and classification proof.

$

Employee Wages & Payroll Costs

MA allows deductions for employee wages and employer-paid payroll taxes. Include benefits like health insurance or paid family leave. File payroll forms like W-2 and M-941. Sole proprietor wages are not deductible.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education & Membership Dues

Workshops, seminars, online classes, and memberships in photography or business associations are deductible. MA matches federal treatment. Document business relevance and keep registration or subscription receipts.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website Costs
--------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising, Promotion & Website Expenses

Print ads, digital promotions, SEO services, web design, and hosting are deductible. MA allows these under “Advertising” with no extra limits. Client gifts up to $25 per person are allowed.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Liability, property, equipment, and auto insurance premiums are deductible if business-related. MA treats these as Schedule C expenses. Workers’ comp premiums are also deductible for those with employees.

$

Licenses, Permit Fees & Professional Taxes

Business certificates, LLC filings, shoot permits, and certification fees are deductible. MA allows deductions on Schedule C, Line 21. Sales tax collected/remitted is not deductible but related fees may be.

$

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props & Set Materials

Props, costumes, and decor used for shoots are deductible. MA treats these as supplies unless very expensive (then depreciate). Keep receipts and notes on usage.

$

Consumable Supplies (Prints, Media & Office Supplies)

Photo paper, ink, USBs, batteries, cleaning wipes, and shipping materials are deductible. MA aligns with federal rules. Track all purchases, including small amounts, for total deduction accuracy.

$

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Other Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Business Loan and Credit Card Interest

Interest from business loans or credit cards is deductible in MA if used for business purposes. Maintain statements and annotate use. Personal loan interest is not deductible.

$

Banking Fees & Payment Processing Charges

Stripe, PayPal, or Square fees, and bank service fees are fully deductible. MA allows these under “Other expenses.” Maintain processor reports and statements.

Other (Niche or State-Specific Deductions)
------------------------------------------

MA-specific deductions and credits

Self-Employment Tax Deduction

MA lets you deduct up to $2,000 of SE tax paid, beyond the federal 50% deduction. Claim on MA Form 1, Schedule Y. Retain Form SE.

$

Self-Employed Health Insurance Premiums

Premiums for yourself, spouse, and dependents are deductible up to your net income. MA follows federal rules. Report on MA Schedule Y. Save Form 1095 or insurer year-end summaries.

$

Abandoned Building Renovation Deduction

Deduct 10% of renovation costs for certified abandoned buildings in designated MA Economic Opportunity Areas. Claim on Schedule C, Line 30. Must obtain EOA certification.

$

Moving Expenses (Relocation for Business)

If you moved to start or grow your business and meet distance/time tests, MA allows moving cost deductions (unlike federal). File using Form 3903 or equivalent worksheet.
