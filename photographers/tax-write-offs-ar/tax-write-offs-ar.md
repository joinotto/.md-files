Lighting, computers, drones

Flashes, strobes, light boxes, drones, computers, monitors, and hard drives are deductible. These can be expensed under Section 179 or depreciated if used for more than one year. Keep invoices and note the date equipment is placed in service.

Accessories and consumables

Memory cards, batteries, chargers, cleaning kits, camera bags, printer ink, and photo paper are deductible in the year purchased as supplies, not depreciated.

Software and subscriptions

Studio or Office Space
----------------------

Deduct expenses for your physical studio or home office used for business.

Studio rent and utilities

Rent for studio or office space, plus utilities (electricity, water, internet, phone) used for business, are fully deductible. This also applies to storage space for props and equipment.

$

Home office deduction

If you use part of your home exclusively and regularly for business, deduct a portion of your rent/mortgage, utilities, and insurance. Calculate this on IRS Form 8829 or use the simplified method. Arkansas follows federal home office rules.

$

Business insurance

Premiums for liability, equipment, and errors & omissions insurance are deductible as business expenses. Health insurance may be deductible elsewhere if you are self-employed.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deduct business travel and transportation costs for shoots and meetings.

Mileage and vehicle expenses

Deduct business mileage at the IRS standard rate ($0.67/mile for 2025) or actual expenses (gas, repairs, insurance, depreciation). Maintain a mileage log and receipts. Commuting from home to your main studio is not deductible.

$

Business travel

Deduct airfare, lodging, rental cars, ride-shares, parking, and 50% of meals for business-related travel (e.g., destination shoots, workshops). Keep receipts and logs for Arkansas audits.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deduct payments to second shooters, editors, and freelance support.

Second shooters and freelancers

Payments to assistants, editors, and second shooters are deductible. Issue Form 1099-NEC for non-employee payments of $600 or more per year. Arkansas follows federal worker classification rules.

Training & Education

Deduct costs to improve your skills and stay current in the industry.

Workshops and courses

Deduct fees for photography workshops, online courses, and conferences that improve your current skills. Travel costs for these events are also deductible.

Professional memberships and publications

Dues for organizations (PPA, ASMP) and subscriptions to trade publications or online learning platforms are deductible if related to your business.

Deduct costs to promote your business and attract clients.

Advertising and website costs

Deduct costs for online ads (Google, Facebook, Instagram), business cards, website design and hosting, SEO services, and printed promotional materials. Client gifts are deductible up to $25 per recipient per year.

Deduct insurance premiums that protect your business and equipment.

Liability and equipment insurance

Premiums for liability, equipment, and errors & omissions insurance are deductible. Health insurance is not a Schedule C expense but may be deductible elsewhere if self-employed.

Licensing & Permits
-------------------

Deduct required permits and fees for legal business operation.

Sales tax permit and shoot permits

Arkansas requires a sales tax permit (fee deductible) for photographers selling tangible goods or taxable services. Permit fees for shooting in public parks or venues are deductible.

Props & Supplies
----------------

Deduct props, backdrops, and consumable supplies.

Props and backdrops

Deduct props, backdrops, and consumables (printer ink, photo paper, packaging) in the year purchased. Use a resale certificate for items resold to clients (e.g., albums, frames) to avoid paying sales tax at purchase.

Financial Expenses
------------------

Deduct business-related bank fees, interest, and processing costs.

Bank fees and loan interest

Deduct business bank account fees, credit card processing fees, and interest on business loans or equipment financing. Arkansas follows federal rules for financial expenses.

Arkansas-Specific Tax Rules
---------------------------

Key Arkansas rules for photographer taxes and compliance.

Sales tax on photography services

Arkansas taxes photography services and tangible goods (prints, albums, USBs) at a 6.5% state rate plus local taxes. Digital files delivered electronically are generally not taxable, but physical products are. If you deliver both digital and physical products, itemize invoices and only charge sales tax on the tangible portion.

$

Section 179 and depreciation

Arkansas conforms to federal Section 179 ($1,220,000 limit for 2025) but does not allow federal bonus depreciation. Adjust your state return if you take bonus depreciation federally
