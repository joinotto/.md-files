Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera, Gear & Computer Purchases

Deduct the cost of business-use cameras, lenses, computers, and gear via depreciation or Section 179. Texas conforms to federal rules, including 80% bonus depreciation. Sales/use tax paid adds to the asset's deductible cost.

$

Equipment Rentals & Leases

Rental fees for lenses, lighting, or studios are fully deductible. Lease-to-own items may require depreciation. Texas taxes rental charges to clients as part of taxable gross receipts, even if equipment is itemized separately.

$

Software & Editing Tools

Subscriptions (Adobe, Lightroom) and purchased software are deductible. Depreciate large software purchases over 36 months or expense via Section 179. Texas follows federal treatment; sales tax is deductible if charged on downloads.

$

Repairs & Maintenance

Expenses for gear maintenance, repairs, cleanings, and replacement parts are deductible in the year incurred. Texas taxes repair services, but you may deduct the full repair cost including sales tax paid.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Home Office Deduction

Claim either $5/sq ft (up to $1,500) or actual home-related costs proportional to workspace. Use Form 8829. Texas mirrors federal treatment and honors full deductions that reduce your Schedule C profit.

$

Studio Rent & Utilities

Rent for commercial studio space and related utilities are fully deductible. Texas doesn't tax commercial rent. Improvements may need depreciation. Keep rental agreements and utility bills for records.

$

Studio Insurance & Security

Business-use insurance (property, liability, renters) and office security system costs are deductible. Allocate only business-use portions. Texas does not tax insurance premiums and accepts full federal deductions.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for business-related travel and transportation

Vehicle Expenses

Choose standard mileage (67¢ per mile in 2024) or actual expenses (gas, repairs, insurance). Keep mileage logs. Texas conforms to federal rules and allows full deduction of business-use vehicle costs.

$

Business Travel

Deduct travel expenses for shoots or conferences: airfare, lodging, 50% meals, taxis, etc. Personal trip portions are non-deductible. Texas follows federal rules—save receipts and note business purpose.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for contract labor and employee expenses

Contract Labor (1099s)

Paying $600+ to second shooters or assistants requires issuing a 1099-NEC. Deduct total paid. Texas mirrors federal treatment and also requires filing the 1099-NEC with the state for resident or in-state contractors.

$

Employee Wages

If hiring staff, wages, payroll taxes, and benefits are deductible. Texas follows federal treatment. Wages to family members may be exempt from some taxes but still deductible—ensure proper payroll compliance.

$

Education & Professional Development
------------------------------------

Deductions for professional development and training

Continuing Education

Workshops, online courses, and conferences to enhance your existing skills are deductible. Pre-business training is not. Texas conforms to federal rules—track expenses and retain course documentation.

$

Professional Memberships & Subscriptions

Dues to groups like PPA and trade magazine subscriptions are deductible. Texas allows full deduction, including sales tax paid. Political/lobbying dues portions (if stated) are not deductible.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website
--------------------------------

Deductions for marketing and online presence

Advertising & Promotions

Print ads, flyers, social media ads, branded swag, and wedding fair booths are deductible. Texas recognizes these as business expenses. Barter deals must be recorded as income/expense at fair value.

$

Website & Online Presence

Hosting, domains, design, portfolio listings, and SEO are deductible. Texas treats web expenses like federal rules and generally does not tax digital services. Deduct full amounts if used for business.

$

Insurance, Licensing & Permits
------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance and permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Deduct premiums for liability, property, gear, or workers' comp coverage. Health insurance for employees is deductible, but personal life insurance is not. Texas mirrors federal treatment and allows full deduction.

$

Licenses, Permits & Regulatory Fees

LLC filings, business licenses, and photography permits are deductible. Texas follows federal rules and allows deduction of regulatory fees. Fines and penalties are not deductible under any condition.

$

Props, Backdrops & Supplies
---------------------------

Deductions for props and studio supplies

Props & Consumables

Deduct backdrops, props, and studio decor under $2,500 immediately. Treat expensive items as assets. Office supplies and consumables like batteries, bulbs, and cleaning tools are also deductible. Texas allows matching deductions.

$

Prints, Albums, & Products (COGS)

Costs for client deliverables (prints, albums) are deductible as COGS or expenses. Texas does not tax resale items; use exemption certificates. Deduct shipping and material costs associated with client orders.

$

Financial Expenses & Loan Interest
----------------------------------

Deductions for financial expenses and loan interest

Bank Fees & Payment Processing

Deduct bank fees, Stripe/PayPal processing fees, and credit card transaction costs. Texas allows full deduction—ensure to separate personal and business accounts. Sales tax does not apply to financial service charges.

$

Business Loan Interest

Deduct interest on loans, credit lines, or financed equipment used for business. Principal is not deductible. Texas does not cap business interest deductions and allows full write-off in line with federal claims.

$

Business Taxes & Fees
---------------------

Deductions for business-related taxes and fees

Business Taxes & Fees

Deduct property taxes on business assets, employer taxes, and regulatory fees. Sales tax on purchases is included in cost. Texas follows IRS rules—income taxes are not deductible, but most business taxes are.

$

Legal & Professional Services
-----------------------------

Deductions for legal and professional services

Legal & Professional Services

Fees to attorneys, CPAs, consultants, and software subscriptions are deductible. Texas allows these deductions in full. Retain engagement letters, invoices, or payment proof for both state and federal compliance.

$

Startup & Organization Costs
----------------------------

Deductions for startup and organization costs

Startup & Organization Costs

Deduct up to $5,000 in startup costs in your first year (amortize the rest over 15 years). Texas mirrors this rule. Costs must be incurred before officially launching your business operations.
