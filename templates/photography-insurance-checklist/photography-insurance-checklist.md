FAQs
----

### Why do photographers need insurance?



Insurance protects photographers from financial loss due to equipment damage, theft, liability claims, or unforeseen business interruptions. It ensures continuity and safeguards against potential legal or financial risks.

### What should be included in a photography insurance checklist?



A checklist should include coverage for equipment, general liability, professional indemnity, cyber liability, business interruption, and workers’ compensation if applicable. It should also track policy details and renewal dates.

### Does photography insurance cover accidental damage?



Yes, many policies include accidental damage coverage, but specifics vary. Photographers should check policy terms to ensure protection against drops, spills, and other accidental incidents.

### Is liability insurance necessary for photographers?



Yes, liability insurance is essential to protect against claims for property damage or injuries occurring during shoots, whether in a studio or on-location.

### Does photography insurance cover stolen equipment?



Most policies cover theft, but photographers should review their policy’s terms, coverage limits, and whether off-site theft is included. Some require proof of forced entry.
