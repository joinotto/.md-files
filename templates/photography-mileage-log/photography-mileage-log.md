FAQs
----

### Why do photographers need a mileage log?



Photographers use a mileage log to document business-related travel for tax deductions, expense tracking, and reimbursement purposes. It ensures accurate records for the IRS or client billing.

### What details should be included in a mileage log?



A complete mileage log should include the date, starting and ending locations, total miles driven, purpose of the trip, and odometer readings.

### Can I deduct mileage for personal photography trips?



No, only mileage related to business activities—such as client shoots, networking events, or location scouting—can be deducted for tax purposes.

### How often should I update my mileage log?



It’s best to update your mileage log after each trip to maintain accurate records and avoid forgetting important details.

### Is there a digital alternative to paper mileage logs?



Yes, many photographers use mobile apps or spreadsheets to log miles, making tracking easier and reducing the risk of losing records.
