FAQs
----

### What are the main types of photo licenses?



Common types include exclusive, non-exclusive, royalty-free, and rights-managed licenses, each determining how a photo can be used and distributed.

### Who owns the copyright in a licensing agreement?



The photographer or original creator retains copyright unless explicitly transferred in writing. A license only grants usage rights, not ownership.

### Can a licensed photo be resold or transferred?



It depends on the agreement. Some licenses allow sublicensing, while others strictly prohibit transferring rights to third parties.

### What should be included in a photo licensing agreement?



Key terms include usage rights, duration, exclusivity, fees, attribution requirements, and any restrictions on modification or distribution.

### How long does a photo license last?



The duration varies and should be specified in the agreement. Some licenses are perpetual, while others expire after a set period.
